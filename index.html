Calling Little Scientists!

We're a research team at the University of Newcastle and we're interested in child development, from infants to toddlers to preschoolers.

Register Your Interest

Screen Time, Sleep & Child Development Study

3- to 5-year-olds
We are investigating preschoolers' screen time (regardless of how much or how little your child uses screens), their sleep, and development.
This study involves:
1. iPad games at the University of Newcastle,
2. A survey about your child's screen use, sleep, behaviour and development,
3. A watch for your child to wear that measures their sleep.

Toddlers' Predictions of Social Interactions

18- to 27-month-olds
We are exploring the types of predictions toddlers are making about social interactions.
This study involves:
A trip to the University of Newcastle where we will track your child's eye movements via an eye tracker while they watch short video clips of cartoon animals playing "hide and seek".

Babies' Perception of People

Up to 10-month-olds
We are interested in where babies look when they see people.
This study involves:
Visiting the University of Newcastle where an eye tracker will monitor your baby's eye movements while your child views a series of images of people.
